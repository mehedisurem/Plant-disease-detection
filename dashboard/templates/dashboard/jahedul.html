Plant Disease Detection using Image Processing

Summary

Identification of plant diseases is key to preventing losses in yield and quantity of agricultural products. The study of plant diseases refers to the study of visually observable patterns that occur in plants. Plant health monitoring and disease detection are crucial for sustainable agriculture. Manually monitoring plant diseases is very difficult. This requires a lot of work, expertise in plant diseases, and additional processing time. Hence, image processing is used for plant disease detection by capturing images of leaves and comparing them with data sets. The data set consists of different plants in image format. In addition to identification, users are directed to an e- commerce website where rates and usage instructions for various pesticides are displayed. This website can be used to efficiently compare the MRP of various pesticides and purchase the one required for the identified disease. This paper aims to support and help greenhouse farmers in an efficient way. The method of Plant Disease Detection using Image Processing typically includes the following steps: 1. Input image: Input images of healthy plants and plants affected by various diseases. These images can be obtained from various sources such as publicly available datasets, field surveys, or laboratory experiments. 2. Preprocessing: The collected images need to be pre-processed to remove noise, correct color balance, and adjust the image size, orientation, and resolution. This step is crucial to ensure that the input images are consistent and suitable for analysis. 3. Segmentation: This phase aims at simplifying the representation of an image such that it becomes more meaningful and easier to Analyze. As the premise of feature extraction, this phase is also the fundamental approach to image processing. There are various methods using which images can be segmented such as k-means clustering, Otsu’s algorithm, thresholding, etc. 4. Feature Extraction: This step involves selecting suitable image processing techniques such as texture analysis, color analysis, and shape analysis to identify features that can differentiate between healthy and diseased plants. 5. Feature Selection: Feature selection can be based on color, shape, and texture. Recently, most researchers are intending to use texture features for detecting plant diseases. 6. Classification: The classification phase implies determining if the input image is healthy or diseased. If the image is found to be diseased, some existing works have further classified it into a number of diseases. A number of classifiers have been used in the past few years by researchers such as k-nearest neighbor (KNN), support vector machines (SVM), artificial neural network(ANN), back propagation neural network (BPNN), Naïve Bayes and Decision tree classifiers. The area of research for a project on Plant Disease Detection using Image Processing lies at the intersection of plant pathology, image processing, and machine learning. The project aims to leverage recent advances in computer vision and machine learning to develop accurate and automated methods for the early detection and diagnosis of plant diseases using image analysis. The use of image processing techniques can help to identify subtle differences in plant features that are indicative of disease symptoms, which can be difficult to detect using traditional methods such as visual inspection. Plant diseases are generally caused by infectious agents such as fungi, bacteria, and viruses. Signs of plant disease are observable evidence of infection and symptoms are the visible effects of these kinds of disease. Fungal infections cause signs like visible spores, mildew, or mold and the basic symptoms are leaf spots and yellowing. So, these are our observations on how to classify the various plant disease and how to be cautious about them. Overall, this work is implemented from scratch and produces a decent accuracy. The future work is to increase the number of images present in the predefined database and to modify the architecture in accordance with the dataset for achieving better accuracy.

Plant Disease Detection and Classification by Deep Learning—A Review

Summary

Plant diseases threaten global food security, causing significant economic losses and reducing crop yields. Early detection and accurate diagnosis of plant diseases are crucial for effective disease management. In recent years, deep learning methods have emerged as a promising approach for plant disease detection and classification. This literature review critically analyzes the existing research on deep learning-based plant disease detection and classification. The research was conducted using keywords such as plant disease detection, plant disease classification,deep learning, and convolutional neural networks. We reviewed studies published in peer-reviewed journals, conference proceedings, and books. The studies were analyzed based on the research questions, methods, results, and conclusions. The review of the literature identified a number of studies that have investigated the use of deep learning for plant disease detection and classification. The studies employed various deep learning techniques such as convolutional neural networks (CNNs), recurrent neural networks (RNNs), and generative adversarial networks (GANs). The studies also utilized different types of image data, including RGB images, hyperspectral images, and thermal images. The results of the studies show that deep learning-based plant disease detection and classification can achieve high accuracy rates, outperforming traditional methods such as visual inspection and laboratory testing. CNNs are the most commonly used deep learning technique, due to their ability to learn complex features and patterns from images. Transfer learning, ensemble learning, and unsupervised learning are the most commonly used deep learning algorithms. However, the studies also highlight some challenges and limitations of deep learning-based plant disease detection and classification. For example, the lack of large and diverse datasets for training deep learning models is a major challenge. In addition, the complexity of deep learning models and the need for significant computational resources can be limiting factors. So, this literature review comprehensively analyzes existing research on deep learning-based plant disease detection and classification. The results of the studies show that deep learning can achieve high accuracy rates in detecting and classifying plant diseases, but there are also challenges and limitations that need to be addressed. Future research should focus on developing large and diverse datasets, improving the efficiency and interpretability of deep learning models, and exploring new deep learning techniques and algorithms for plant disease detection and classification.

Summary

Agriculture is very important in human life. About 60% of the population is directly or indirectly engaged in some form of agriculture. There is no conventional technology to detect various crop diseases in an agricultural environment, due to which farmers are not interested in increasing their agricultural productivity day by day. Crop diseases affect the growth of their respective species, so early detection is important. Many machine learning (ML) models have been used to detect and classify crop diseases, but with recent advances in a subset of ML, deep learning (DL), this area of research appears to hold much promise in terms of improved accuracy. The proposed method uses a convolutional neural network and a deep neural network to effectively and accurately detect and identify crop disease symptoms. Furthermore, multiple efficiency metrics are used to evaluate these techniques. This article provides a thorough description of DL models that are used to visualize crop diseases. Furthermore, a number of research gaps have been identified that could lead to greater clarity in plant disease detection, even before symptoms appear. The proposed approach aims to develop a convolution neural network-based technique for plant leaf disease detection.

Summary

Agriculture plays an important role in human life. About 60% of the population is engaged in specific agriculture directly or indirectly practice. Nowadays, however, farmers due to this, cultivation has stopped and shifted to other sectors less adoption of automation and other factors such as growing demand for farm work. Farmers are therefore increasingly dependent on the introduction of cognitive alternatives to take advantage of technological change. In the field of smart precision farming, Arduino and the Internet of Things together create new dimensions. This proposed approach aims to develop a method of detecting plant conditions based on Temperature, Humidity by a DHT11 sensor as well as a Soil Moisture sensor and TCS3200 color sensor used to detect Plant leaves colors for the application of soil. The method combines IoT with Arduino by considering various features such as color, soil moisture, temperature, and various sensors to help. So, it can recognize leaf diseases in plants.

Plant Disease Detection Using Image Processing and Machine Learning

Summary

Plant diseases can cause significant losses to crop yields and quality. Traditional methods of detecting plant diseases, such as visual inspection, can be time-consuming and prone to errors. Therefore, there is a growing interest in developing automated systems for plant disease detection. In recent years, image processing and machine learning techniques have been applied to detect plant diseases accurately and quickly. The project will involve the following steps: 1. Dataset Preparation: Collect a dataset of plant images with different types of diseases and normal plants. The dataset will be labeled with the corresponding disease type. 2. Image Processing: Pre-processing the plant images using techniques such as noise reduction, segmentation, and feature extraction. These pre-processing steps will help in extracting meaningful information from the images and reducing noise. 3. Machine Learning: Developing a machine learning model to classify plant images into different disease types. Different classifiers such as support vector machines, decision trees, and deep neural networks will be trained and evaluated to find the best-performing model. 4. System Integration: Integrating image processing and machine learning algorithms to create an end-to-end system for plant disease detection. The system will take an input image and output the detected disease type and its severity. The final system will provide an accurate and fast method for detecting plant diseases, which can help farmers take timely action to prevent the spread of diseases and increase crop yield. The project will also help in overcoming the challenges of data availability, variations in imaging conditions, and identification of multiple diseases in a single plant.
