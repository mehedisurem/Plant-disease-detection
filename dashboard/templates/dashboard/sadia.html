Densenet based model for plant diseases diagnosis

Summary

Plant diseases pose the biggest risk to the safety of food. The biggest problem facing the agricultural industries is the identification of plant diseases. Computer vision problems involving image classification can be successfully handled by convolutional neural networks (CNN). A transfer learning-based model for spotting diseases in plant leaves is presented in this study. This paper proposes DenseNet201, a CNN classifier built on a transfer learning model. An examination of four deep learning models (VGG16, Inception V3, ResNet152V2, and DenseNet201) was conducted to determine which one is most accurate at spotting plant diseases. A web-based application was created to diagnose plant diseases using faulty leaf images and a proposed model that can both identify the disease and suggest a course of action. The used images dataset includes 28310 leaf photos from three different crops—tomato, potato, and pepper—divided into 15 different classes, including 9 classes for tomato disorders and 1 class for healthy conditions, 2 classes for potato disorders and 1 class for healthy conditions, and 1 class for pepper disorders. The proposed model, according to the results of our experiment, has the highest training accuracy of 99.44% and validation accuracy of 98.70%.

Reference:

  • Venkatesh, Y N, S ST, S S, Hegde SU. Transfer Learning based Convolutional Neural Network Model for Classification of Mango Leaves Infected by Anthracnose. In: 2020 IEEE International Conference for Innovation in Technology (INOCON). 2020: 1–7

Technological support for detection and prediction of plant diseases

Summary

Technological advancements have greatly improved the detection and prediction of plant diseases, providing farmers with more efficient ways to manage their crops. Here are some of the technological tools and approaches that are commonly used in this field: Remote Sensing: Remote sensing technology, such as hyperspectral imaging, can detect subtle changes in plant physiology caused by diseases, allowing for early detection and diagnosis. Machine Learning: Machine learning algorithms can be trained to identify patterns and symptoms of plant diseases from images, allowing for accurate and efficient diagnosis. DNA-Based Methods: DNA-based methods, such as polymerase chain reaction (PCR), can be used to detect the presence of pathogens in plant samples, enabling quick and accurate diagnosis. Internet of Things (IoT): IoT devices can monitor various environmental factors that affect plant health, such as temperature, humidity, and soil moisture. These devices can provide real- time data to farmers, allowing for early detection and prevention of diseases. Mobile Apps: Mobile apps are available that can help farmers identify and diagnose plant diseases based on visual symptoms. These apps can also provide recommendations for treatment and management. Overall, technological advancements have greatly improved the detection and prediction of plant diseases, allowing for more efficient and effective management of crops.

Reference:

  • 1.Agrios, G.N., 2005. Plant pathology. 5th Edition: Elsevier Academic Press. Burlington, Ma. USA. pp.79–103.
